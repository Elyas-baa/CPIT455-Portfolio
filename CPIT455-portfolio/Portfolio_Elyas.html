Tawakkalna ‚Äì Dependability Mapping

I mapped the five dependability attributes to Tawakkalna as a national digital identity and health verification system used by citizens, residents, and government authorities.

Dependability Attributes

  • Availability: The system must be accessible 24/7 because users rely on immediate verification to access public services and facilities.
  • Reliability: The application must provide accurate identity and health status information without errors.
  • Safety: Incorrect verification results could lead to denied access or incorrect decisions, so the system must avoid harmful outputs.
  • Security: Since Tawakkalna handles sensitive personal and medical data, strong encryption and access control are required.
  • Resilience: The system must handle high traffic and recover quickly from failures or network disruptions.

Risks and Failure Sources

Risks may occur from:

  • Infrastructure failures: server overload, network outages.
  • Software failures: bugs in verification logic or data synchronization errors.
  • Operational failures: incorrect configuration or human mistakes.
  • Security threats: unauthorized access attempts or cyberattacks.

Dependability Strategy

To maintain dependability, Tawakkalna applies layered protection:

  • Fault prevention: secure coding practices and input validation.
  • Fault tolerance: load balancing and redundant servers to avoid single points of failure.
  • System protection: encryption and multi-factor authentication.
  • Recovery mechanisms: backup systems and automatic failover to restore services quickly.

Tawakkalna ‚Äì Dependability Strategy (Availability Focus)

System Profile (Users & Usage Pattern)

The selected real-world system is the Tawakkalna application, a national digital service used by citizens, residents, and government authorities in Saudi Arabia for identity and health status verification. The system operates in a high-demand environment where millions of users access it daily, especially when entering public facilities, completing travel procedures, or performing government transactions. Its usage pattern is continuous and real-time, as users depend on immediate verification results. For this reason, the system is considered uptime-critical, since any service interruption directly affects public operations and prevents users from completing essential activities.


Metric Selection (POFOD vs ROCOF vs AVAIL)

Based on this system profile, the most appropriate dependability metric is Availability (AVAIL). Tawakkalna‚Äôs primary requirement is to remain operational whenever verification is requested. Unlike systems where the probability of failure on demand (POFOD) is the main concern, or systems evaluated by failure frequency (ROCOF), Tawakkalna requires continuous accessibility. If the system becomes unavailable, users cannot verify their identity or health status, leading to service disruption. Therefore, maximizing uptime and minimizing downtime represents the key engineering objective.


Proposed Strategy (Architectural Decision)

To support high availability, a fault-tolerant architectural strategy is proposed. The system utilizes distributed servers combined with load balancing to handle heavy traffic and eliminate single points of failure. If one server becomes unavailable, other servers continue processing requests without interrupting service. In addition, a caching mechanism allows temporary access to user data during network disruptions. This layered approach ensures continuous operation and directly enhances system availability even when infrastructure faults occur.
